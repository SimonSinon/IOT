The Challenge

Warehouse Transformation
for B2B2C

The move to B2B2C changes the shape of demand. Volatility is amplified, SKU portfolios explode, and the same warehouse must handle both bulk pallets and piece-level picking.

Why It Matters Now

B2C demand is sensitive to promotions, holidays, and external drivers such as weather. This volatility is amplified when the SKU portfolio grows rapidly, because planners must make frequent decisions across thousands of SKU-channel combinations.

Multiple pack-sizes complicate FIFO execution, location assignment, and returns quarantine. Even a good forecast is insufficient if the organization cannot rapidly translate insights into coordinated actions.

This challenge requires not only analytics, but also collaboration mechanics: a shared data layer, shared metrics, shared alerts, and an auditable decision workflow.

Root Causes

Current Pain Points

The most critical bottleneck is not the lack of effort — it is the absence of a shared operational truth and a shared decision loop.

Fragmented Data Sources

When procurement, production, shipping, and warehouse each maintain different spreadsheets, the organization spends time reconciling numbers rather than acting. By the time a decision is aligned, the situation has already changed.

Static Safety Stock

Fixed safety stock formulas do not scale when SKU counts triple and demand volatility increases. The right inventory buffer depends on forecast uncertainty, lead-time variability, and channel-specific service targets — not a static parameter revised too slowly.

Warehouse Space Constraints

Limited capacity becomes fragile under high SKU variety, mixed order profiles, and returns handling. Without a forward-looking capacity view, reactive moves like temporary overflow and emergency re-slotting increase congestion.

Slow Exception Diagnosis

Root causes may sit in supplier lead time, production sequencing, inbound scheduling, putaway capacity, or outbound dispatch cutoffs. Without end-to-end visibility and structured case management, escalation is manual and slow.

Core Capabilities

AI-Powered Modules

From demand sensing to space forecasting, SynchroTower converts data into coordinated, executable recommendations.

📈

Demand Sensing

Near-term 7-day demand sensing blending historical data with calendar effects and external drivers like weather — especially relevant for beverages where temperature impacts demand.

🎯

Dynamic Safety Stock

Adaptive safety stock targets per SKU and channel that adjust as the model detects higher uncertainty from promotions, weather spikes, or supply variability. No more static parameters.

🏗️

Space Forecasting

Converts projected inventory into space consumption using SKU dimensions and storage-type mapping. Produces forward-looking capacity pressure views by zone with early warnings.

🔄

Coordinated Recommendations

Cross-functional recommendations reflecting procurement, production, and warehouse constraints. Generated as "cases" with explanations, suggested actions by owner, and expected impact.

🚨

Exception Alerting & RCA

Continuous monitoring for stockouts, inbound delays, and space saturation. Traces event timelines to attribute root causes — transparent, rule-based logic upgradeable to ML classification.

📋

Unified Data Layer

Standardized SKU master data with pack-size conversion and physical dimensions. Creates an event timeline for end-to-end traceability and faster root-cause diagnosis across the chain.
